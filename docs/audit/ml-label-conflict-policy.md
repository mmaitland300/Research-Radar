# ML Label Conflict Policy

This policy defines how Research Radar treats duplicate paper labels, conflicting labels, blind-source labels, and future split eligibility before further learned-model experiments. It applies to `ml-label-dataset-v4` and later audit label exports unless a later policy supersedes it.

## Scope

- This policy covers offline manual label artifacts only.
- It does not change labels, dataset artifacts, ranking code, API behavior, or production defaults.
- It does not create train/dev/test splits.

## Observation-Level vs Paper-Level Labels

Current v4 artifacts are observation-level audit data. A row represents one reviewed paper in one review context, with worksheet provenance and any ranking context preserved for audit use.

Duplicate `paper_id` rows are allowed when they come from different review contexts, including rank-shaped, contrastive, gap, and blind-source worksheets. These rows may reflect different reviewer questions or selection mechanisms.

Do not silently merge duplicate paper rows. Any paper-level view must be produced by an explicit policy or artifact that states how duplicate observations were grouped and how labels were aggregated or filtered.

## Conflict Handling

Raw label conflicts and derived-target conflicts must be reported. A raw conflict occurs when the same `paper_id` has multiple non-empty values for the same manual label field across observations. A derived-target conflict occurs when deterministic derived targets disagree for the same `paper_id`.

Conflicts are not automatically resolved. They may reflect context differences, ambiguous papers, labeling drift, or genuine reviewer disagreement.

Learned experiments must state which conflict policy they use:

- use observation-level rows as separate examples;
- exclude conflicting papers or conflicting targets;
- apply an explicit paper-level aggregation policy.

The chosen policy must be recorded with the experiment artifact. Silent de-duplication, first-row wins, last-row wins, or score-driven conflict resolution are not allowed.

## Blind-Source Labels

`ml_blind_snapshot_audit` rows remain `family=null`. They were not sampled as family-selected top-k recommendation labels.

Blind-source labels may be used for source-split diagnostics. They must not be treated as family-selected recommendation labels.

Ranking context fields on blind rows, including family scores, family ranks, cluster fields, topics, and abstract previews, are context/provenance only, not labels. They must not be used to infer labels or to relabel the row family.

## Train/Dev/Test Eligibility

The current split remains `audit_only`. No train/dev/test split exists yet.

Any future split must be generated by a dedicated command and artifact. The artifact must include:

- split seed;
- source-policy;
- leakage checks;
- duplicate/conflict policy;
- source-slice counts;
- artifact version and command provenance.

Future split generation must avoid paper leakage across train/dev/test when paper-level evaluation is intended. If observation-level splits are used, the artifact must state why duplicate `paper_id` rows can safely cross split boundaries or prove that they do not.

## Recommended Near-Term Experiment Policy

A source-split tiny baseline may train on rank-shaped emerging labels and evaluate on blind-source labels only as offline diagnostics.

Metrics from this split are not validation and must not change production ranking defaults. Results must be reported by source slice, including at minimum rank-shaped training rows and blind-source diagnostic rows.

Experiments using this policy must state:

- the target label or derived target;
- whether rows are observation-level or paper-level;
- how duplicate and conflicting labels were handled;
- which source slices were used for training and diagnostic evaluation;
- that the result is offline diagnostic material only.

## Required Caveats

This policy does not validate Research Radar ranking quality.

Manual labels remain single-reviewer offline audit material unless a source explicitly says otherwise.

Duplicate and conflicting labels are evidence management concerns, not errors to silently erase.

No production ranking change is supported by this policy.
